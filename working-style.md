# Working Style & Decision Making

*Last updated: 2026-07-13*

## What Makes a Good Day
A day where Or maximized efficiency — did important things, not just urgent ones. Made progress toward personal and professional goals. Not just reactive.

## Biggest Time Drains to Eliminate
- Bureaucracy
- Research for purchases (e.g. finding the right car, product comparisons)
- Social media
- Anything that pulls attention away from important vs. urgent work

## Decision Making Under Competing Priorities
1. Handle what is genuinely urgent first
2. Delegate urgent-but-not-important tasks
3. Reserve personal focus for what is truly important
- Goal: stay in "important" work, not just "urgent" work

## Hard to Say No To
- Requests from Nitzan or Sergey (senior leadership) — Or acts on these fast
- Tasks that are exciting and energizing
- Tasks that close a long-open loop
- Tasks that deliver high value quickly

## Energy & Focus
- **Not a morning person.** Sharpest in the evening.
- Deep work best done in evening blocks.
- Afternoons: async, reviews, lighter tasks around school pickup window.

## Delegation Instinct
- Strong. Or actively delegates urgent-but-not-important work to preserve focus for high-leverage tasks.

## Platform Habits
- Opens Slack first every morning (primary work communication at monday.com)
- monday.com for task management
